Tim Kim's video "How I Learned to Code in 4 Months & Got a Job! (No CS Degree, No Bootcamp)" is a great inspiration for anyone who wants to learn to code. Kim shares his personal story of how he went from being a college dropout with zero technical skills to landing a software developer job in just 4 months.

Kim's advice is simple but effective:

Instead, focus on learning the core concepts of programming, such as variables, functions, loops, and data structures. Be consistent with your learning. It's better to study for a little bit every day than to cram for a few hours once a week. Set aside some time each day to practice coding, even if it's just for 30 minutes. Build projects. The best way to learn to code is by building projects. This will help you apply what you're learning and see it in action. Start with small projects, such as a simple calculator or website. As you gain more experience, you can start working on more complex projects. Kim also emphasizes the importance of networking with other developers. Once you have a basic understanding of coding, start reaching out to other developers and asking for their advice and mentorship. Networking can help you learn new things, find job opportunities, and build a community of support. Overall, Tim Kim's video is a great resource for anyone who wants to learn to code. His advice is simple, effective, and actionable. If you're serious about learning to code, I highly recommend watching his video.

Here are some additional thoughts on Kim's video:

Kim's approach to learning to code is very self-directed. He didn't attend a coding bootcamp or take any formal classes. Instead, he learned to code by reading books and articles, watching online tutorials, and working on personal projects. This approach is not for everyone, but it can be a great way to learn to code if you're self-motivated and disciplined. Kim also emphasizes the importance of failure. Finally, Kim's video is a reminder that it is possible to learn to code and get a job in the tech industry without a computer science degree or a coding bootcamp. How to work effectively in a Team

Chris White's TEDxAtlanta talk on how to create a work culture that brings out the best in employees is a must-watch for any leader. White argues that by unblocking communication, becoming responsive, and aiming higher, leaders can harness all of the energy and creativity that employees have to offer.

Unblock communication

White begins by talking about the importance of unblocking communication in the workplace. He argues that leaders need to create an environment where employees feel comfortable speaking up and sharing their ideas. This means being open to feedback, even if it is negative. It also means being willing to listen to employees and take their concerns seriously.

Become responsive

White also emphasizes the importance of being responsive to employees. He argues that leaders need to be quick to respond to emails, phone calls, and other communications from employees. This shows that leaders value employees' time and input. It also helps to build trust and rapport between leaders and employees.

Aim higher

Finally, White encourages leaders to aim higher for their teams. He argues that leaders should set ambitious goals and challenge their employees to achieve them. This helps to motivate employees and keep them engaged in their work.

I agree with White that communication, responsiveness, and ambition are essential for creating a work culture that brings out the best in employees. I believe that leaders who focus on these areas will be able to create teams that are more productive, innovative, and successful. I particularly like White's emphasis on unblocking communication. I believe that it is important for employees to feel comfortable speaking up and sharing their ideas, even if they are different from the leader's ideas. This is how leaders can learn new things and make better decisions. I also like White's advice about being responsive to employees. I believe that employees appreciate it when leaders take the time to listen to them and respond to their concerns. This shows that leaders value employees and care about their well-being. Finally, I agree with White that leaders should aim higher for their teams. I believe that employees are more motivated and engaged when they are working towards ambitious goals. Leaders can help employees to achieve their full potential by setting challenging goals and providing them with the resources and support they need to succeed. Overall, I think that White's talk is a valuable resource for leaders who want to create a work culture that brings out the best in their employees. I encourage all leaders to watch it.

How to Take Notes in Meetings Effectively" by Asana

This article provides a comprehensive overview of how to take notes in meetings effectively. The author, Asana, is a leading provider of work management software.

The article begins by emphasizing the importance of taking notes in meetings. It explains that good notes can help you to remember what was discussed, track action items, and make decisions. The article also provides a number of tips for taking effective notes, including:

  • Prepare for the meeting: Before the meeting, take some time to review the agenda and familiarize yourself with the topics that will be discussed. This will help you to focus your note-taking and to identify the most important information.
  • Use a note-taking template: Using a note-taking template can help you to stay organized and to ensure that you are capturing all of the important information. There are a number of different note-taking templates available online, or you can create your own.
  • Focus on the key points: Don't try to write down everything that is said in the meeting. Instead, focus on capturing the key points, such as decisions that were made, action items that were assigned, and important questions that were raised.
  • Review your notes: After the meeting, take some time to review your notes and to fill in any gaps. You may also want to add your own insights and reflections.

The article also provides a number of additional tips, such as using abbreviations and symbols, reviewing your notes regularly, and sharing your notes with others.

Overall, this article is a great resource for anyone who wants to improve their note-taking skills. The tips that are provided are clear, concise, and actionable. I highly recommend reading this article if you want to learn how to take more effective notes in meetings.
